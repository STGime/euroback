Hi,

Sorry — the TLS certificate for *.eurobase.app expired earlier today. Your project's API endpoint (and every other tenant subdomain on the wildcard) refused TLS handshakes for roughly 50 minutes, from 11:11 to ~12:00 UTC.

If your app showed a connection or "couldn't load" error during that window, that's why. No data was lost; nothing on your side needs fixing. Once the new certificate was issued, traffic resumed automatically.

What happened

Auto-renewal of the wildcard certificate had been silently failing for the past month against a misconfigured DNS solver — and we weren't alerting on the failure. So instead of the renewal landing quietly at day 60, the cert ran out the clock.

What we're doing so it can't happen again

  • The renewal path is fixed — the new cert is valid through September 22 and will renew on its own.
  • Adding an hourly in-cluster certificate-expiry check that pages our on-call to Discord if anything is < 14 days from expiry. The next outage of this shape is one we hear about in advance, not at 11:11.
  • Shipping a TLS-expiry runbook so the next responder doesn't have to retrace the diagnosis.

Beta or not, your customers' app showing an error is on us. Thanks for your patience today, and reply to this email if you saw any tail-end weirdness we should look at.
